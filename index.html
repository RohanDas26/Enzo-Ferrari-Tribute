The Beautiful Creations

"The most beautiful car is the one that wins." These five cars represent the absolute pinnacle of engineering, passion, and design under Enzo's influence and in his honor.

250 GTO (1962)

The crown jewel of Ferrari. Built for homologation into the FIA's Group 3 Grand Touring Car category, it dominated its class. Powered by the legendary 3.0L Colombo V12 engine producing 300 horsepower, it was as beautiful as it was fast. Today, it stands as the most valuable and highly sought-after car in automotive history.

Testarossa (1984)

An absolute cultural icon of the 1980s. Featuring its unmistakable side strakes (often called "cheese graters") and a massive naturally aspirated 4.9L flat-12 engine, it became the poster car for an entire generation. It was a symbol of ultimate luxury, excess, and raw speed.

F40 (1987)

The very last car personally approved by Enzo Ferrari before his death. Built to celebrate Ferrari's 40th anniversary, it was a raw, aggressive, twin-turbocharged V8 monster. Featuring a Kevlar, carbon fiber, and aluminum body, it stripped away all luxury in pursuit of speed. It was the first production car to break the 200 mph barrier.

F50 (1995)

Built to celebrate the 50th anniversary, the F50 was essentially a Formula 1 car for the road. It featured a naturally aspirated 4.7L V12 derived directly from the 1990 Ferrari 641 F1 car. The engine was bolted directly to the carbon-fiber tub as a stressed member, offering an incredibly visceral, uncompromising driving experience.

Enzo Ferrari (2002)

Named in honor of the company's founder as the ultimate tribute. The "Enzo" was the pinnacle of early 2000s hypercar technology. With a carbon-fiber body utilizing extreme ground-effect aerodynamics (negating the need for a large fixed rear wing), an F1-style electrohydraulic shift transmission, carbon ceramic brakes, and a howling 6.0L V12, it captured Il Commendatore's relentless pursuit of perfection.
